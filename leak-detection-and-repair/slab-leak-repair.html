Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a slab leak include unexplained increases in water bills, damp or warm spots on floors, low water pressure, the sound of running water when no taps are open, and cracks in the walls or foundation.

Slab leaks are detected using specialized equipment and techniques such as acoustic listening devices, pressure testing, infrared cameras, and electronic leak detection to pinpoint the exact location of the leak.

Slab leaks can be caused by various factors including poor construction, corrosion of pipes, shifts in the foundation, high water pressure, and chemical reactions between metals and soil.

Repairing a slab leak is a complex task that requires professional expertise. It involves locating the leak accurately and fixing it using specific methods, which are generally outside of typical DIY skills.

The cost of repairing a slab leak can vary significantly depending on the location and severity of the leak, the method of repair, and the labor costs in your area. On average, costs can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars.
