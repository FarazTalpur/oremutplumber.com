Why Choose Sewer Camera Inspection?

Sewer camera inspections are an advanced method for diagnosing plumbing problems without unnecessary digging or disruption. This non-invasive technique utilizes high-resolution cameras to capture detailed footage of your plumbing system, revealing blockages or damages with precision.

Benefits of Sewer Camera Inspection

  • Accurate Diagnosis: Pinpoint exact locations of clogs, leaks, or tree root infiltration.
  • Time-Efficient: Quick identification saves time, preventing prolonged damage.
  • Cost-Effective: Early detection reduces repair costs by addressing issues promptly.
  • Minimal Disruption: Avoids unnecessary excavation, preserving your landscape.

Sub-Services Offered

Pipe Blockage Detection

Using cutting-edge camera technology, technicians can identify even the smallest clogs caused by grease build-up, hair, or foreign objects. Avoid the mess and hassle typical of traditional methods with a more precise approach.

Leak Detection and Repair

Leaks that go unnoticed can cause significant structural damage. With camera inspections, you can locate leaks hidden within walls or underground, facilitating timely and effective repairs.

Tree Root Intrusion Solutions

Roots naturally seek out moisture, making sewer lines a common target. The camera inspection reveals root intrusions, allowing for strategic removal and preventing further damage.

Assessment of Old Pipes

Inspect aging pipes for corrosion or wear before they become serious issues. Our experts provide detailed reports to help you make informed decisions about pipe replacement or maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

A sewer camera inspection is a process where professionals use a specialized waterproof camera to look inside sewer lines and underground pipes to locate blockages, cracks, or other issues without needing to dig up the pipes.

A sewer camera inspection can help identify and locate problems in your sewer lines before they become major issues, preventing costly repairs and ensuring your plumbing system functions efficiently.

It's advisable to conduct a sewer camera inspection every 1-2 years, especially if your home is older, or if you experience frequent plumbing issues. New homebuyers may also benefit from an inspection before finalizing their purchase.

While sewer camera inspections can detect a wide array of issues such as clogs, pipe collapses, root intrusion, and corrosion, not all problems may be visible, especially if the issue is within the pipe walls or due to complex underground plumbing layouts.

The duration of a sewer camera inspection can vary, but it typically takes about 30 minutes to an hour to complete, depending on the length and complexity of your sewer lines.
