Frequently Asked Questions

It's recommended to check your swimming pool plumbing for leaks at least once a month, especially during the swimming season. Regular inspections can help identify minor issues before they become major problems.

Common signs include a sudden drop in water level, soft or wet spots around the pool area, higher than usual water bills, and air bubbles in the pool return lines.

Minor leaks in accessible areas can sometimes be fixed by homeowners with some DIY skills, but more complex leaks, especially those involving underground pipes, should be handled by professionals to ensure accurate diagnosis and repair.

Regularly clean skimmer and pump baskets, backwash the filter as needed, avoid overloading the pool with debris, and schedule periodic professional inspections to ensure optimal flow and prevent clogs.

Yes, professional maintenance is important at least annually, as experts can identify potential issues, provide thorough cleaning, and ensure the plumbing system is operating efficiently, reducing the risk of costly repairs.
