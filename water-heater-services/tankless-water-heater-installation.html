Frequently Asked Questions

Tankless water heaters provide endless hot water, higher energy efficiency, and take up less space compared to traditional tank-style heaters. They also have a longer lifespan and can reduce your energy bills over time.

To determine the correct size, consider the peak hot water demand in your household, measured in gallons per minute (GPM). Calculate the GPM required by adding the flow rates of all hot water devices that may run simultaneously.

Yes, a tankless water heater can replace a traditional tank water heater. However, the installation might require adjustments to your existing plumbing and electrical systems to handle the different requirements of a tankless model.

Installation generally requires proper venting, an appropriate gas line or electrical circuitry, and potentially an upgrade to your existing utility connections. It's recommended to have a professional assess your home for compatibility and make the necessary installations.

The cost of installing a tankless water heater varies based on factors such as the model chosen, complexity of the installation, and regional labor rates. Typically, it can range from $1,000 to $3,500 including installation.
